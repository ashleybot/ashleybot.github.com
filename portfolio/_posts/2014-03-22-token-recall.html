Recall

This assignment was centered around research on memory recall. Our focus was to find a solution for the workplace.

The problem space seemed very clear to us. What do people tend to forget at work? Meetings.

The Token

A token is printed during a meeting. Each attendee is given their own print afterward.

During the meeting, the attendee can mark a moment as being important (the black bands). Since the tokens are printed in real time, the entire token acts as a timeline for the meeting.

A black band then helps to recall specific memories in the context of the meeting's progression.

The other colors can serve as recall devices for meeting content. For example, the attendee may prefer to customize the color by project.

Meetings

Each meeting has a token of a unique landmark. Attendees therefore have more identifiers to recall a meeting than simply the date and time.

The Eiffel Tower, Big Ben, Great Pyramid, or any recognizable landmark can be used.

The chronocological ordering of meetings is then visualized as a trip around the world. Another mechanism to help with recall.

The Interface

The token itself is used as a controller to an interface to view information from a meeting.

It can be turned and twisted to reveal more detailed topics, events, and important facts.
