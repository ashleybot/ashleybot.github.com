I'm really excited about this post. It combines several things I enjoy: outdoor activities, data visualization, Ruby, and Javascript.

This weekend I ran in the Golden Gate Trail Run half marathon. It was the longest distance I have ever ran and it was nothing short of amazing.

The results are posted online, but are a little hard to consume. So, I scrubbed the page with a fast little Ruby parser called Nokogiri, saved the results as XML, and used XSLT to display as HTML under the "Raw" tab. [Insert joke about web developers fixing things that have already been fixed here.]

  • Simple
  • Lightweight
  • Raw
  • The tabs are clean and simple unordered-list markup and basic CSS.
  • The tabs are cross-browser, but don't need a ton of hacky CSS or markup.

  • Raw race results.
